Zimeng Tan

I am a sixth-year Ph.D. student in the Department of Automation at Tsinghua University, advised by Prof. Jianjiang Feng and Prof. Jie Zhou. Prior to that, I received my B.S. degree in the Department of Automation at Beijing Institute of Technology in 2019.

My research interests lie in computer vision and deep learning, particularly in medical image anaylysis and vascular intelligence analysis.

Publications

Multi-Scale Multi-Attention Network for Structured Vascular Mesh Reconstruction
Zimeng Tan, Zhanqiang Guo, Jianjiang Feng, and Jie Zhou
submitted to IEEE Transactions on Medical Imaging (TMI), 2024

We propose a modeling algorithm that converts the vascular surface to a structured vertex-consistent mesh and a multi-scale multi-attention network (MSMANet) for structured vascular mesh reconstruction.
Cross-Domain Cerebrovascular Landmark Detection via Contrastive and Adversarial Framework
Zimeng Tan, Zhanqiang Guo, Jianjiang Feng, and Jie Zhou
submitted to IEEE Transactions on Medical Imaging (TMI)
(Invited Submissions from ISBI 2023 Best Papers), 2024

We propose a semi-supervised domain adaptation method for cerebrovascular landmark detection, which captures domain-invariant prior information from cross-domain data.
Multi-Task Global Optimization-Based Method for Vascular Landmark Detection
Zimeng Tan, Jianjiang Feng, Wangsheng Lu, Yin Yin, Guangming Yang, and Jie Zhou
Computerized Medical Imaging and Graphics (CMIG), 2024
[PDF][Dataset] [Code (coming soon)]

We propose a multi-task global optimization-based framework for accurate vascular landmark detection, where the spatial relationships between landmarks are utilized explicitly to tackle the landmark confusion problem.
3D Vascular Segmentation Supervised by 2D Annotation of Maximum Intensity Projection
Zhanqiang Guo, Zimeng Tan, Jianjiang Feng, and Jie Zhou
IEEE Transactions on Medical Imaging (TMI), 2024 [PDF] [Code]

We propose a weakly supervised segmentation framework based on maximum intensity projection (MIP) annotations.



Adversarial Learning Framework for Cerebrovascular Landmark Detection Using Cross-Modality Information
Zimeng Tan, Jianjiang Feng, Wangsheng Lu, Yin Yin, Guangming Yang, and Jie Zhou
IEEE International Symposium on Biomedical Imaging (ISBI, Best Paper), 2023 [PDF]

We propose an adversarial learning framework for cerebrovascular landmark detection by lever aging cross-modality information.
Cerebrovascular Landmark Detection under Anatomical Variations
Zimeng Tan, Jianjiang Feng, Wangsheng Lu, Yin Yin, Guangming Yang, and Jie Zhou.
IEEE International Symposium on Biomedical Imaging (ISBI, oral), 2022 [PDF]

We propose a deep learning network for vascular landmark detection that is robust to anatomical variations.
SGNet: Structure-Aware Graph-Based Network for Airway Semantic Segmentation
Zimeng Tan, Jianjiang Feng, and Jie Zhou
International Conference on Medical Image Computing and Computer-Assisted Intervention (MICCAI), 2021 [PDF]

We propose a structure-aware graph-based network (SGNet) for airway semantic segmentation from chest CT scans, which integrates structural prior information by introducing irregular edges.
Multi-Task Learning Network for Landmark Detection in Anatomical Tree Structures
Zimeng Tan, Jianjiang Feng, and Jie Zhou
IEEE International Symposium on Biomedical Imaging (ISBI, oral), 2021 [PDF]

We propose a multi-task network for anatomical landmark detection in anatomical tree-like structures that regresses heatmap confidence map, semantic segmentation, and orientation field in parallel.
A Cascade Regression Model for Anatomical Landmark Detection
Zimeng Tan, Yongjie Duan, Ziyi Wu, Jianjiang Feng, and Jie Zhou
Statistical Atlases and Computational Models of the Heart (STACOM), 2019 [PDF]

A two-stage cascade regression model is proposed to perform coarse-to-fine landmark detection.
